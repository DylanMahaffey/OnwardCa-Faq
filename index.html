Frequently Asked Questions

Who We Are

What Is Bitwise?

Bitwise Industries is a tech ecosystem, activating human potential to elevate underdog cities across the country. In other words: we’re all about bridging the gaps between individuals and the resources they need to be successful. To get a better idea of what we’re all about, watch this video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P9CZN7C9pwI

We’re so happy to have you involved! And remember- nobody deserves to be here more than you do

What Is Onward?

Onward is an initiative aiming to connect displaced workers across the country to essential services and careers in their area. This initiative was created in direct response to the COVID-19 pandemic, and is projected to help millions of displaced workers get back on their feet as quickly as possible, jumpstarting economic recovery in the process.

You can think of Onward as a branch of Bitwise, but this is a huge initiative powered through partnerships with many different organizations.

Who Do I Work For?

In this situation, you are an Independent Contractor. Bitwise is your customer, hiring you for your services (Data Entry). You will keep track of your own hours, up to but not exceeding 40 hours per week, and invoice Bitwise for those hours through our system.

Invoicing

How Do I Set Up My Pay?

Step 1: Submit your W-9 form

  • You’ll do this through an online form sent to you by our team, so that our accountants can easily and quickly input your data into our system.
  • After submitting this form, you’ll receive a unique ID number that you will need to put on your invoices.
Step 2: Set up your account on Bill.com
  • Our team will send you a link to do this.
  • Once you set up your account on Bill.com, you will not need to access it again, as we will not be invoicing through Bill.com. You will submit your invoice via an online form that our team sends you.

I’m Confused About What To Put On My Bill.com Profile

If you’re new to the world of being an Independent Contractor, it can be confusing. Here’s some information to help clarify some things:

  1. As an Independent Contractor offering your Data Entry services, you are your own Company, and you’re the Company Owner as well. Company’s Name will be your name and Company Owner’s SSN will be your SSN.
  2. Our accounting team doesn’t need you to fill out the fields Business Type, Industry, and Accounting Service but Bill.com requires them for your profile. So it doesn’t matter too much, but we recommend the following:
    • Business Type: Individual
    • Industry: General Service
    • Accounting Software: I don’t use an accounting software

What If I Already Have a Bill.com Account?

If you already have an account set up with Bill.com for past work, please make sure your billing information is up to date. Then, you’ll need to access the link sent to you by our leadership team so that Bitwise is linked to your account as a customer.

Important Note: You will not invoice us through Bill.com, even if that’s what you’re used to. You will use the online form sent to you by our team to make things go smoother on our end.

How Will I Invoice You?

Our team will send you a secure online invoice form and remind you when it’s time to submit it. Remember to save the unique ID number you received after submitting your W-9, as this will need to be entered on all invoices.

You’ll keep track of your hours worked on your own, on whatever platform works for you. Remember to communicate to your leadership team which time of day you’ll be working, so they can easily cross-check your hours on our software if needed.

If I’m Keeping Track Of My Own Hours, Who’s Validating Them For Me?

Your leadership team takes on this responsibility, and they’ll be checking to see if your hours invoiced realistically line up with your work completed and time spent on our system. If any issues concerning this arise, they will be reaching out to you for clarity.

It’s important to communicate to your Resource Coordinator which hours you’ll be working, so that these cross-checks go smoothly when needed and there’s no hold put on your invoice.

How Often Will I Get Paid?

We will be on an every-other-week pay schedule, and your leadership team will remind you when it’s time to send in your invoice for each pay period.

Tip: As an Independent Contractor, you are strongly encouraged to save 25-30% of each paycheck, to go towards your taxes at the end of the year. First-time Independent Contractors who don’t know this often run into trouble when it comes time to pay taxes, because they find themselves financially unprepared.
